# 🎓 MIS & IT Operations Course Curriculum Guide

This documentation provides a comprehensive overview of the curriculum, learning modules, labs, and interactive scenarios for the three academic tracks implemented in the portal:
1. **🖥️ BSc Computer Science / IT Track**
2. **📊 BSc Accounts (Accounting) Track**
3. **👔 MBA (Business Administration) Track**

---

## 📂 Course Overview
This semester-long program is split into foundational IT operations (Weeks 1 & 2) followed by a simulated survival scenario (Week 3 onwards) designed to test systems design, resource management, and administrative capacities under extreme constraints.

---

## 🖥️ Week 1: Virtualization & Resource Management

### Track Customizations:
*   **BSc Computer Science / IT:**
    *   *Lecture:* Understanding Type 1 (Bare-Metal e.g., Proxmox VE, ESXi) vs. Type 2 (Hosted e.g., VirtualBox) Hypervisors.
    *   *Reference Library:* Curated ISO library including Ubuntu Desktop/Server, Debian, and Alpine Linux configurations.
    *   *Interactive Tool:* Hypervisor Performance Comparison Simulator.
*   **BSc Accounts:**
    *   *Lecture:* Cost-Benefit analysis of virtualization, shifting CapEx to OpEx, and setting up resource chargeback models.
    *   *Interactive Tool:* Virtualization Cost-Savings & ROI Calculator.
*   **MBA:**
    *   *Lecture:* Strategic cloud migration, hybrid deployment governance, risk mitigation, and Vendor SLA management.
    *   *Interactive Tool:* Cloud vs. On-Premises Deployment Strategic Scorecard.

---

## 💾 Week 2: Physical Infrastructure & Bootable Systems

### Track Customizations:
*   **BSc Computer Science / IT:**
    *   *Lab:* Bootable USB utilities (Rufus, BalenaEtcher, Ventoy), partition styles (GPT/MBR), and boot targets (UEFI/BIOS).
    *   *Hardware:* CPU (cores vs. clock speed), volatile RAM limits, and non-volatile storage (NVMe vs. SATA HDDs).
    *   *Interactive Tool:* Flashing duration estimator based on ISO size and write speeds.
*   **BSc Accounts:**
    *   *Lab:* Spreadsheet verification controls (SHA-256 validation), backup security audits, and access controls for physical storage units.
    *   *Hardware:* Straight-line depreciation schedules, lifecycle hardware replacements, and salvage value estimations.
    *   *Interactive Tool:* Asset straight-line depreciation schedule simulator.
*   **MBA:**
    *   *Lab:* Authoring Business Continuity Plans (BCP) and Disaster Recovery Plans (DRP), focusing on Recovery Time Objectives (RTO) and Recovery Point Objectives (RPO).
    *   *Hardware:* Strategic procurement planning, avoiding vendor lock-in, scalability planning, and Green Computing (ESG) metrics.
    *   *Interactive Tool:* Business Downtime Financial Risk Estimator.

---

## ☢️ Week 3: Post-Apocalyptic Survival & Resource Coordination Sandbox

**Scenario Premise:** The year is 2028. Following 6 months of a global conflict, the conventional power grid is 100% offline. A population of 5M+ survivors has coalesced near local water basins. All operations rely on scavenged resources and low-power hardware.

### Track Customizations:
*   **BSc Computer Science / IT:**
    *   *Syllabus:* Mesh LAN designs, low-power server architecture, and server failovers.
    *   *Simulator:* Power availability metrics based on solar panels, and water purification volume capacities.
*   **BSc Accounts:**
    *   *Syllabus:* Inventory audits, tracking physical resources, and ledger reconciliation under resource-scarce environments.
    *   *Simulator:* Resource inventory shrinkage calculator.
*   **MBA:**
    *   *Syllabus:* Executive crisis decision-making, strategic resource prioritization, and organizational stability under constraints.
    *   *Simulator:* Resource allocation simulator (Comms vs. Water vs. Security) and predicted population stability outcomes.
